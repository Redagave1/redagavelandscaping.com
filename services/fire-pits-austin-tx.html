Fire Feature Construction

Fire That Defines the Space

A fire pit turns a backyard into a destination. In Austin, where evening temperatures drop just enough to make a flame feel perfect from October through April, a custom fire feature extends your outdoor living season by months and becomes the natural gathering point for every event. Red Agave designs and builds fire pits that go well beyond the prefabricated rings sold at hardware stores. We custom-weld steel fire bowls, build floating fire pits that appear to hover over water or gravel, and construct masonry fire features with integrated seating walls.

We work with both gas and wood-burning configurations. Gas fire pits use a dedicated line run from your meter with a key valve or electronic ignition for instant on-off control. We fill gas pits with volcanic rock, tumbled lava stone, or reflective black fire glass depending on the look you want. Wood-burning pits are built with spark screens and proper ventilation for safe, code-compliant operation. The City of Austin allows both gas and wood-burning fire pits on residential properties, but wood-burning pits must be at least 15 feet from any structure and comply with Travis County burn ban regulations. We design every fire pit to meet these codes from the start.

Most custom fire pit projects in Austin range from $4,000 to $18,000 depending on design complexity, materials, and whether the fire feature is integrated into a larger hardscape project or outdoor kitchen. View our Signature Projects to see completed fire features in action.

Common Questions

Fire Pit FAQ

Are fire pits legal in Austin, TX?
Yes. Both gas and wood-burning fire pits are allowed on residential properties in Austin. Gas fire pits have no seasonal restrictions. Wood-burning fire pits must be at least 15 feet from any structure and are subject to Travis County burn ban days during drought conditions. We design every fire pit to meet City of Austin fire code requirements from the start.
Gas or wood burning — which is better?
It depends on your priorities. Gas fire pits offer instant ignition, no smoke, no ash cleanup, and no burn ban restrictions. They are ideal for entertaining and tight spaces close to the house. Wood-burning fire pits provide the traditional campfire experience with crackling sounds and the smell of real wood. They cost less to install but require more maintenance and have seasonal restrictions during burn bans.
How much does a custom fire pit cost in Austin?
Custom fire pit projects in Austin typically range from $4,000 to $18,000. A simple gas fire bowl with volcanic rock starts around $4,500. A custom-welded Corten steel floating fire pit with seating walls and integrated lighting runs $10,000 to $18,000. We provide free on-site estimates and can design a fire feature for any budget.

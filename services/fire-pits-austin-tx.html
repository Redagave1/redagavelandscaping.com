Fire Feature Construction

Fire That Defines the Space

A fire pit turns a backyard into a destination. In Austin, where evening temperatures drop just enough to make a flame feel perfect from October through April, a custom fire feature extends your outdoor living season by months and becomes the natural gathering point for every event. Red Agave designs and builds fire pits that go well beyond the prefabricated rings sold at hardware stores. We custom-weld steel fire bowls, build floating fire pits that appear to hover over water or gravel, and construct masonry fire features with integrated seating walls.

We work with both gas and wood-burning configurations. Gas fire pits use a dedicated line run from your meter with a key valve or electronic ignition for instant on-off control. We fill gas pits with volcanic rock, tumbled lava stone, or reflective black fire glass depending on the look you want. Wood-burning pits are built with spark screens and proper ventilation for safe, code-compliant operation. The City of Austin allows both gas and wood-burning fire pits on residential properties, but wood-burning pits must be at least 15 feet from any structure and comply with Travis County burn ban regulations. We design every fire pit to meet these codes from the start.

Most custom fire pit projects in Austin range from $4,000 to $18,000 depending on design complexity, materials, and whether the fire feature is integrated into a larger hardscape project or outdoor kitchen. View our Signature Projects to see completed fire features in action.

Gas vs Wood Fire Pit in Austin

Choosing between gas and wood-burning is the first decision in any fire pit project. Both have real advantages depending on your property, your HOA, and how you use your outdoor space. Here is how they compare across the factors that matter most in Austin.

Cost Range$5,000–$15,000$3,000–$8,000
Fuel Cost$5–$15/evening$20–$40/cord (lasts ~20 fires)
Startup TimeInstant (flip a switch)15–20 minutes
SmokeNoneYes (check HOA rules)
Heat OutputConsistent, adjustableVariable, hotter
MaintenanceAnnual gas line inspectionAsh cleanup after each use
AmbianceClean flame, no crackleFull fire experience with crackle and smoke

Most Austin HOAs allow gas fire pits but restrict wood-burning. If you are in a neighborhood with HOA restrictions, gas is typically your only option. We recommend gas for properties where the fire pit sits close to the house or under a pergola, and wood-burning for open backyard settings where smoke dispersal is not an issue.

Which Is Right

Fire Pit vs Outdoor Fireplace

Fire Pits offer 360-degree seating, a more social and communal atmosphere, and a lower cost range of $3,000–$15,000. They can be added to existing patios with minimal structural work and offer more versatile placement options across your property.

Outdoor Fireplaces serve as a focal wall piece with directional heat. They carry a higher cost range of $15,000–$35,000, require masonry and structural work, and create a defined "room" feel that anchors an outdoor living space.

Most Austin backyards are better suited to fire pits because they encourage circular seating arrangements and do not require structural walls. Outdoor fireplaces work best when you are building a full outdoor living room with a covered structure. We build both — schedule a consultation to discuss which fits your space.

Common Questions

Fire Pit FAQ

Are fire pits legal in Austin, TX?
Yes. Both gas and wood-burning fire pits are allowed on residential properties in Austin. Gas fire pits have no seasonal restrictions. Wood-burning fire pits must be at least 15 feet from any structure and are subject to Travis County burn ban days during drought conditions. We design every fire pit to meet City of Austin fire code requirements from the start.
Gas or wood burning — which is better?
It depends on your priorities. Gas fire pits offer instant ignition, no smoke, no ash cleanup, and no burn ban restrictions. They are ideal for entertaining and tight spaces close to the house. Wood-burning fire pits provide the traditional campfire experience with crackling sounds and the smell of real wood. They cost less to install but require more maintenance and have seasonal restrictions during burn bans.
How much does a custom fire pit cost in Austin?
Basic fire pit projects start at $3,000–$5,000 for a steel fire bowl on a gravel pad with gas connection. Mid-range projects with a custom stone or concrete surround, cap stone, and integrated lighting run $5,000–$12,000. Premium fire features with seating walls, water features, and full hardscape start at $12,000 and can reach $25,000+. Gas line installation adds $800–$2,000 depending on distance from the meter. We provide free on-site estimates.
Do I need a permit for a fire pit in Austin?
The City of Austin does not require a permit for portable or permanently installed fire pits that are at least 15 feet from any structure. Gas fire pits require a licensed plumber for the gas line connection. If your fire pit is part of a larger outdoor living project with structural elements (seating walls, pergolas), those elements may require a building permit. We handle all permitting as part of our design-build process.
Can you add a fire pit to my existing patio?
Yes. We frequently add fire pits to existing concrete patios, flagstone patios, and deck areas. For gas fire pits on existing patios, we trench the gas line under or alongside the patio and connect to your existing gas meter. The fire pit itself sits on or is built into the existing surface. Most retrofit fire pit projects complete in 2–4 days.
What's the best fire pit material for Austin weather?
Mild steel and concrete both perform well in Austin's climate. Steel fire bowls develop a natural rust patina that many clients prefer, or we can powder-coat them for a clean finish. Concrete fire pits handle the heat cycling without cracking when properly reinforced. We do not use aluminum — it does not hold up to the sustained heat of a fire pit.
Gas fire pit vs fire table — what's the difference?
A fire pit is typically round or square with an open flame. A fire table combines a gas burner with a functional tabletop surface, usually with a center fire trough. Fire tables are great for dining areas or smaller patios where the fire feature needs to double as a table. We build both.

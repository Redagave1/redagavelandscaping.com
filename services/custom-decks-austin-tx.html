Deck Construction

Elevated Outdoor Living

A deck is the foundation of outdoor living in Austin. It defines the space, creates elevation changes that make small yards feel larger, and provides a clean, level surface for furniture, grills, and entertaining. Red Agave builds custom decks using three material families, each selected to handle Central Texas conditions: Dekorators composite decking, Western red cedar, and Brazilian IPE hardwood. The right choice depends on your budget, how much maintenance you want to do, and the aesthetic you are after.

Dekorators composite boards are our most popular option for Austin builds. They resist UV fading, do not splinter, and never need staining or sealing. The Voyage line offers realistic wood grain textures in colors that pair well with the Hill Country palette. Cedar is the classic choice. It is naturally resistant to rot and insects, lighter than hardwoods, and takes stain beautifully. It does require re-staining every 2 to 3 years to maintain its color in the Austin sun. IPE is the premium tier. This Brazilian hardwood is so dense it sinks in water, resists rot, insects, and fire, and lasts 50 years or more with minimal maintenance. We source FSC-certified IPE and can leave it to silver naturally or oil it annually to maintain its deep brown tone.

Every deck we build includes steel post brackets set in concrete footings, proper joist spacing for the decking material, and hidden fastener systems for a clean surface with no visible screw heads. We integrate pergolas, privacy walls, built-in bench seating, and FCOB under-rail lighting as part of the structure. Most custom deck projects in Austin range from $15,000 to $80,000+. Materials

Deck Materials for Austin TX

01
IPE Hardwood
The gold standard. Brazilian hardwood rated 3,680 lbs on the Janka scale. Naturally rot, insect, and fire resistant. Weathers to silver or maintains rich brown with annual oiling. 25+ year lifespan.
02
Western Red Cedar
Classic grain and warm color. Naturally resistant to decay and insects. Lighter than IPE, easier to work with for complex designs. Needs stain/seal every 2-3 years. 15-20 year lifespan.
03
Composite (Trex/TimberTech)
Engineered wood-plastic composite. Zero maintenance, no staining or sealing required. Consistent color, won't splinter. 25-year manufacturer warranty. Higher upfront cost, lower lifetime cost.
04
Thermally Modified Wood
Kiln-treated at 400°F+ to remove moisture and sugars. Dimensional stability in Austin's heat cycling. No chemical preservatives. 20+ year lifespan.
05
Mild Steel Framing
We frame every deck with mild steel instead of pressure-treated lumber. Steel doesn't warp, rot, or attract termites. It creates a perfectly level, rigid structure that will outlast the decking material itself.
06
Hidden Fasteners
All our decks use hidden fastener systems. No visible screws on the walking surface. Clean lines, no catching on bare feet, and a more finished appearance.

IPE vs Composite vs Cedar in Austin

Choosing the right decking material comes down to three factors: how you want it to look, how much maintenance you are willing to do, and your budget. Here is how the three main options compare in Austin's climate.

Feature IPE Hardwood Composite Western Red Cedar
Cost (per sq ft, installed) $45-$65 $35-$55 $25-$40
Lifespan 25-40 years 25-30 years 15-20 years
Maintenance Annual oiling None Stain/seal every 2-3 years
Heat Retention Moderate High (can be hot barefoot) Low
Appearance Rich natural grain Consistent manufactured look Warm natural grain
Scratch Resistance Excellent Good Fair
Austin Climate Rating Excellent Excellent Good

In Austin's climate, IPE and composite are our top recommendations. Both handle the 100+ degree summers and UV exposure without warping or fading. Cedar is a beautiful choice but requires more ongoing maintenance to look its best. We help every client choose the right material based on budget, aesthetic preference, and how much maintenance they want to do.

Common Questions

Deck FAQ

Composite or wood — which is better for Austin?
For most Austin homeowners, composite is the better long-term value. It handles UV exposure, heat, and moisture without fading, warping, or requiring annual staining. Dekorators composite boards stay cool enough to walk on barefoot even in July. Cedar is the right choice if you prefer a natural wood aesthetic and do not mind re-staining every 2 to 3 years. IPE is the premium choice for clients who want a 50-year deck with unmatched hardness and grain character.
How much does a custom deck cost in Austin?
Most custom deck projects in Austin range from $15,000 to $80,000+ depending on size, material, and features. Entry-level decks (200-300 sq ft, composite or cedar on a mild steel frame) start at $15,000-$25,000. Mid-range builds (300-500 sq ft, IPE or premium composite with built-in seating and lighting) run $25,000-$45,000. Premium multi-level IPE decks with full landscape integration run $45,000-$80,000+. All decks are built on mild steel frames with design, engineering, permits, and installation included.
IPE or composite — which is better for Austin?
Both perform excellently in Austin's climate. IPE is the premium choice: it's the hardest wood available, naturally resists everything Austin throws at it, and has an unmatched natural grain. The tradeoff is annual oiling to maintain the brown color (or let it weather to silver). Composite is the zero-maintenance choice: no staining, no sealing, consistent color for 25+ years. The tradeoff is a manufactured look and higher surface temperature in direct sun. Most of our clients choose based on whether they want a natural wood aesthetic or true zero maintenance.
Do I need a permit for a deck in Austin?
Yes. The City of Austin requires a building permit for any attached deck and for freestanding decks over 200 sq ft or 30 inches above grade. We handle all permitting as part of our design-build process, including structural engineering drawings, site plans, and inspections. Permit fees are included in our project pricing.
How long does it take to build a deck?
Most residential decks take 2-4 weeks from groundbreaking to completion. Simple single-level decks can be completed in 10-14 days. Large multi-level decks with complex railing, lighting, and landscape integration take 3-4 weeks. The design and permitting phase adds 2-4 weeks before construction begins.
Why do you use steel framing instead of wood?
Mild steel frames don't warp, rot, split, or attract termites. In Austin's heat, pressure-treated lumber can twist and cup within a few years, creating an uneven deck surface. Steel stays perfectly flat and level for decades. It also allows longer spans without intermediate posts, giving you a cleaner look under the deck. The steel frame will outlast any decking material we put on top of it.

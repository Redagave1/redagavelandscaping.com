Xeriscape Design

Water-Wise Landscapes That Look Incredible

Austin's Stage 2 water restrictions are not a temporary inconvenience. They are the new normal in a region where population growth consistently outpaces water supply. Xeriscape is not about sacrificing beauty for conservation. Done right, a xeriscape yard is more visually striking than a conventional lawn, requires a fraction of the water, and needs almost no maintenance once established. Red Agave specializes in xeriscape and hardscape design that transforms Austin yards into landscapes that thrive in 105-degree heat without irrigation systems running around the clock.

We use native and adapted Texas plants exclusively. Flame Acanthus provides hummingbird-attracting red tubular blooms from spring through fall. Blackfoot Daisy delivers continuous white flowers on a compact, spreading form that never needs deadheading. The Basics

What Is Xeriscaping?

Xeriscaping is landscaping designed to reduce or eliminate the need for supplemental watering. The term was coined in Denver in 1981 by combining "xeros" (Greek for dry) with "landscaping." It is built on seven principles: planning and design, soil improvement, practical turf areas, efficient irrigation, mulching, appropriate plant selection, and proper maintenance. Xeriscaping does not mean rocks and cacti. It means choosing the right plants for your climate and designing the landscape so water is used with precision rather than waste.

Austin is one of the best cities in the country for xeriscape. Stage 2 water restrictions are effectively permanent, summers regularly exceed 100 degrees for weeks at a time, and the native caliche clay soil drains poorly and challenges conventional turf. A properly designed xeriscape uses 50-75% less water than a conventional lawn. Austin Water's WaterWise rebate program provides up to $2,500 for qualifying lawn replacements, making the transition even more accessible. The native plants that thrive here are not compromise choices. They are some of the most beautiful, resilient species available anywhere.

The upfront cost of xeriscaping is higher than laying sod, but the long-term economics are not close. Here is how the two approaches compare for a typical Austin homeowner over five years.

Annual Water Bill $1,200-$2,400 $200-$600
Annual Maintenance $2,400-$4,800 $400-$800
5-Year Total Cost $21,000-$41,000 $10,000-$21,000
Water Usage 25,000+ gal/year 5,000-10,000 gal/year
Maintenance Time 2-4 hrs/week 1-2 hrs/month

Xeriscape costs more upfront but pays for itself within 2-3 years through water savings and eliminated lawn maintenance. Most Austin homeowners see a 50-60% reduction in their monthly water bill after converting to xeriscape.

Common Questions

Xeriscape FAQ

How much does xeriscaping cost in Austin, TX?
Most xeriscape projects in Austin range from $8,000 to $35,000 depending on yard size, materials, and plant selection. A typical 1,000 sq ft xeriscape with decomposed granite, native plants, and steel edging runs $12,000 to $18,000. We provide free on-site estimates for every project and can design for any budget.
Will a xeriscape yard look dry and barren?
Not at all. A well-designed xeriscape is more colorful and textured than a conventional lawn. Native Texas plants like Flame Acanthus, Salvia Greggii, and Blackfoot Daisy bloom continuously from spring through fall. Mexican Feathergrass adds flowing movement. Combined with decomposed granite in warm tones and natural stone, the result is a landscape that looks intentional and alive year-round.
Does Austin offer rebates for removing grass?
Yes. Austin Water offers the WaterWise Landscape Rebate program, which provides up to $2,500 for qualifying residential properties that remove traditional turf and replace it with water-efficient landscaping. We can help you navigate the rebate application process and design a landscape that qualifies. The rebate can offset a significant portion of your xeriscape investment.
How long does xeriscape installation take?
Most residential xeriscape projects take 1-3 weeks from start to finish. Smaller front yards can be completed in 3-5 days. Larger whole-yard transformations with flagstone, boulders, and lighting typically take 2-3 weeks. We schedule a start date during your design approval and provide daily progress updates.
Can I still have some grass with xeriscape?
Absolutely. Xeriscape is about using water wisely, not eliminating all green. Many of our clients keep a small functional turf area for kids or pets and surround it with native planting beds and decomposed granite. We use Zoysia or Bermuda for these areas since they handle Austin's heat better than St. Augustine and require less water.
What maintenance does a xeriscape yard need?
Very little. Native plants need supplemental water only during their first summer. After establishment, most need zero irrigation. Maintenance is limited to cutting back perennials once in late February, occasional weeding (much less than lawn), and refreshing decomposed granite every 2-3 years. No mowing, no fertilizing, no fungicide.
Will xeriscaping increase my home value?
Yes. The National Association of Realtors reports that professional landscaping adds 10-15% to home value. In Austin specifically, water-wise landscapes are increasingly attractive to buyers concerned about long-term water costs and restrictions. A well-designed xeriscape also has stronger curb appeal than a stressed, brown lawn.
Do you work with the Austin WaterWise rebate program?
Yes. Austin Water's WaterWise Landscape Rebate offers up to $2,500 for qualifying lawn conversions. We help clients navigate the application process, which requires before photos and a landscape plan showing at least 50% of irrigated turf being replaced. Our designs are structured to maximize your rebate eligibility.

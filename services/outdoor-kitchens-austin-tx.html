Outdoor Kitchen Construction

Cook, Entertain, Live Outside

Austin's outdoor entertaining culture runs year-round. With 300 days of sunshine and a food scene that treats the backyard grill as seriously as any restaurant kitchen, the outdoor kitchen has become the centerpiece of luxury living in Central Texas. Red Agave designs and builds custom outdoor kitchens that go far beyond a standalone grill on a patio slab. We build permanent, weather-resistant cooking and entertaining stations with built-in grills, smokers, side burners, stone or granite countertops, stainless steel refrigeration, ice makers, and integrated bar seating.

Every outdoor kitchen starts with a masonry or steel-framed island built on a proper footing. We use natural stone veneer, stacked limestone, or stucco finishes depending on the style of your home. Countertop options include polished granite, Dekton, and concrete. We handle all gas line work, electrical runs for lighting and appliances, and plumbing for sinks and ice makers. Most outdoor kitchen builds in Austin range from $20,000 to $75,000 depending on size, appliance selection, and finish materials. Larger projects that include pergola coverage and integrated fire features can run higher.

We coordinate with licensed plumbers and electricians as part of the build, and we pull all required permits through the City of Austin. From concept sketches to the first dinner party, most outdoor kitchen projects take 4 to 8 weeks. Schedule a free consultation to start designing yours.

Common Questions

Outdoor Kitchen FAQ

How much does an outdoor kitchen cost in Austin?
Most custom outdoor kitchens in Austin range from $20,000 to $75,000 depending on island size, appliance selection, countertop material, and finish work. A compact L-shaped kitchen with a built-in grill, small fridge, and granite countertop starts around $22,000. Full kitchens with smokers, sinks, ice makers, and bar seating run $45,000 and up. We provide detailed estimates during your free consultation.
Do I need a gas line for an outdoor kitchen?
For built-in gas grills and side burners, yes. We coordinate with licensed plumbers to run a dedicated gas line from your meter to the outdoor kitchen island. This is included in our design-build process and covered under permit. Propane conversions are also available if natural gas is not accessible at your property.
What countertop materials work best outdoors in Texas?
Granite and Dekton are the most popular choices for outdoor kitchens in Austin. Both resist UV fading, heat damage, and staining. Granite offers a natural stone look and is available in hundreds of colors. Dekton is an ultra-compact surface that is virtually indestructible. Poured concrete is another option for a more industrial aesthetic and can be custom-colored to match your design.

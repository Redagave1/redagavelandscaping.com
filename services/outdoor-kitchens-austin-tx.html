Outdoor Kitchen Construction

Cook, Entertain, Live Outside

Austin's outdoor entertaining culture runs year-round. With 300 days of sunshine and a food scene that treats the backyard grill as seriously as any restaurant kitchen, the outdoor kitchen has become the centerpiece of luxury living in Central Texas. Red Agave designs and builds custom outdoor kitchens that go far beyond a standalone grill on a patio slab. We build permanent, weather-resistant cooking and entertaining stations with built-in grills, smokers, side burners, stone or granite countertops, stainless steel refrigeration, ice makers, and integrated bar seating.

Every outdoor kitchen starts with a masonry or steel-framed island built on a proper footing. We use natural stone veneer, stacked limestone, or stucco finishes depending on the style of your home. Countertop options include polished granite, Dekton, and concrete. We handle all gas line work, electrical runs for lighting and appliances, and plumbing for sinks and ice makers. Most outdoor kitchen builds in Austin range from $15,000 to $75,000 depending on size, appliance selection, and finish materials. Larger projects that include pergola coverage and integrated fire features can run higher.

We coordinate with licensed plumbers and electricians as part of the build, and we pull all required permits through the City of Austin. From concept sketches to the first dinner party, most outdoor kitchen projects take 4 to 8 weeks. Schedule a free consultation to start designing yours.

Why Red Agave

One Contractor, One Vision

Most outdoor kitchen projects require at least three contractors: a kitchen builder for the island, a concrete company for the patio, and a landscaper for everything else. Each contractor works from their own plan, their own timeline, and their own idea of what "done" looks like. The result is a kitchen that sits on a patio that sits in a yard — three separate projects stitched together.

Red Agave designs and builds the entire outdoor living space as one project. The kitchen, patio, fire feature, planting, drainage, and lighting are designed together from day one. The concrete slab is poured with the kitchen island footprint and gas line trenches already planned. The stone veneer matches the patio material. The lighting plan accounts for both task lighting at the grill and ambient lighting in the seating area. One team, one timeline, one vision. That's the design-build advantage.

Common Questions

Outdoor Kitchen FAQ

How much does an outdoor kitchen cost in Austin?
Outdoor kitchen costs in Austin range from $15,000 to $75,000+. Essential grill islands start at $15,000–$25,000. Complete L-shaped or U-shaped kitchens with refrigeration, sink, and bar seating run $25,000–$45,000. Premium full kitchens with multiple appliances, integrated hardscape, and lighting run $45,000–$75,000+. Prices include design, materials, masonry, countertops, appliance installation, gas, electrical, and plumbing. Appliances are selected by the client and purchased separately or through our vendor accounts.
Do I need a gas line for an outdoor kitchen?
For built-in gas grills and side burners, yes. We coordinate with licensed plumbers to run a dedicated gas line from your meter to the outdoor kitchen island. This is included in our design-build process and covered under permit. Propane conversions are also available if natural gas is not accessible at your property.
What countertop material is best for outdoor kitchens in Austin?
Granite and poured concrete are our top recommendations for Austin outdoor kitchens. Granite is heat-resistant, scratch-resistant, and handles Austin's UV exposure without fading. Poured concrete allows custom shapes, integrated sinks, and custom colors, but requires sealing annually. We also work with soapstone for clients who want a more natural, matte finish. We do not recommend marble or tile — marble stains and etches from food acids, and tile grout degrades in Austin's freeze-thaw cycles.
How long does it take to build an outdoor kitchen?
Most outdoor kitchens take 3-6 weeks from groundbreaking to completion. Simple grill islands can be completed in 2-3 weeks. Full outdoor kitchens with custom masonry, multiple appliances, and integrated hardscape take 4-6 weeks. The design phase typically takes 2-3 weeks before construction begins. Total timeline from first consultation to first cookout is usually 6-10 weeks.
Do I need a permit for an outdoor kitchen in Austin?
Outdoor kitchens with gas, electrical, and plumbing connections require permits in Austin. The gas line requires a plumbing permit, electrical work requires an electrical permit, and if the kitchen includes a roof structure (pergola or pavilion), a building permit is needed. We handle all permitting as part of our design-build process — it's included in the project cost.
Can you add an outdoor kitchen to my existing patio?
Yes, if the existing patio slab is thick enough (minimum 4 inches of reinforced concrete). We assess the existing slab during consultation. If it needs reinforcement, we can pour a supplemental pad for the kitchen island. Gas and electrical lines are trenched alongside the existing patio to the meter and panel.
What appliances do you recommend?
We work with any appliance brand but most frequently install grills from Bull, Blaze, and Lynx. For refrigeration, we recommend Blaze and Bull outdoor-rated units. The key is choosing appliances rated for permanent outdoor installation — standard indoor appliances will fail within a year in Austin's heat and humidity. We can provide appliance packages through our vendor accounts at contractor pricing.

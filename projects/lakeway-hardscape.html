The Brief · Lakeway

Stone that moves.

1,800 sq ft · 16 Days

The backyard was 1,800 square feet of bare caliche and patchy Bermuda — baking in full sun, draining nowhere, connecting to nothing. The homeowners wanted a single unified surface that could handle a 12-person dinner party, a fire pit gathering, and daily family use without looking like a parking lot.

The answer was marbled concrete: two integral colors — charcoal and warm sand — hand-swirled during the pour so the veining runs through the full 4-inch slab depth. No topcoat to peel. No stamps to wear flat. The marbled finish upgraded the base $14/sq ft concrete to $22/sq ft, but it eliminated the need for pavers at $21/sq ft with none of the settling, shifting, or weed infiltration that comes with a segmental system on expansive clay.

Every edge is defined by 1/4-inch mild steel — 240 linear feet welded and set on-site — separating concrete from planting bed, concrete from decomposed granite, concrete from turf. The steel edging doubles as a channel for 24V FCOB lighting, turning every border into a continuous glow line after dark.

The Build

Poured. Swirled. Set in steel.

Hardscape · 01 The Slab Marbled · Board-Formed Edges 1,800 sq ft poured concrete on 6-inch compacted limestone base. Rebar on 18-inch centers. 4-inch minimum slab thickness with thickened edges at grade transitions. Two integral colors hand-swirled during the pour. Board-formed edges using rough-sawn cedar formwork. Control joints saw-cut at 10-foot intervals within 24 hours of pour.

Steel Edging + Fire Pit

  • —Corten weathering finish — natural rust patina

Lighting + Controls

  • —24V FCOB embedded in steel edging channels — 240 lf continuous

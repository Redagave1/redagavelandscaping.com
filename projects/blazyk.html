Design Intent · Circle C, Austin

Steel carved the grade. Fire and glass gave it a pulse.

Steel · Stone · Fire · Glass · Timber

The yard dropped away from the house with nothing to hold it — just bare ground and a live oak. We cut the slope into five distinct planes: three terraced steel areas stepping down through gravel and boulders, and two terraced deck levels cantilevered off the house. Quarter-inch steel edges every transition, giving each level a clean, deliberate horizon line against the natural grade.

Two fire features anchor the upper terrace. A stone fire pit sits low in the gravel court, surrounded by timber-slat benches and boulders. A second stone-and-steel fire pit breaks the edge between deck and garden. Both burn against a backdrop of turquoise glass — two cantilevered steel planters filled with crushed turquoise glass and underlighting that turns them electric at dusk. An Anacacho Orchid Tree rises from the largest planter, the glass glowing beneath it.

The planting palette softens every steel edge without hiding it. Blue Fescue, River Fern, Variegated Sedge, and White Cloud Muhly fill the terraced beds alongside boulders. Russian Sage, Common Tree Senna, Sky Pencil Holly, White Drift Rose, Wooly Stemodia, Mexican Sage, Leopold Plant, and Webberville Sedge complete the layers — thirteen species that read wild and textured against the precision of the steel.

The Build

Steel, stone, fire. Five terraces.

Steel · 01 Terraced Steel 1/4" Steel · Three Levels Quarter-inch steel edges carve the natural slope into three distinct terraced areas. Each level holds its own composition of gravel, boulders, and ornamental grasses — Blue Fescue, Variegated Sedge, White Cloud Muhly. The steel reads as a single continuous material from top to bottom, giving the entire grade change an architectural rhythm.
Steel Meets Stone
Three terraces. Two fire features.
Night · 01

After Dark

LED Strips · Hanging Lanterns

At night the geometry reveals itself. LED strips trace every steel edge and deck transition in amber light, drawing the terraced structure against the darkness. Hanging lanterns in the live oak canopy wash the branches from below. The entire composition reads differently after sundown — the steel disappears and the light takes over.

Glass · 02

Turquoise Glow

Cantilevered Steel · Crushed Glass

Two cantilevered steel planters hold crushed turquoise glass over integrated underlighting. By day the glass catches the sun in fractured teal. At night it glows electric green-blue against the amber deck strips. The contrast between warm fire light and cool glass is the signature of this build.

Fire · 03

Dual Fire

Stone Pit · Stone + Steel Pit

Two fire features work the upper terrace. The stone fire pit sits low in the gravel court, ringed by timber-slat benches and boulders — intimate, contained. The second stone-and-steel fire pit breaks the transition between deck and garden, a hybrid form that bridges the two material languages.

Design Intent · Austin, TX

Steel, fire, and a plant palette built for the Texas climate.

Pavers · Steel · Fire · Xeriscape

This Austin backyard took shape around a pool under construction. Rather than extending the existing concrete patio to the water, we broke the pattern — large format pavers poured with synthetic turf joints in between, giving the pool deck a rhythm the original slab never had.

The yard is terraced with 1/4-inch welded steel edges that step the grade in clean planes. Two custom floating steel fire pits anchor different zones — one long and low in amber fire glass on the back lawn, one square and bold in aqua glass at the pool edge. Both are fabricated in-house, welded to float off the ground on a recessed base.

Behind the pool, bermuda sod fills the open lawn with Texas hardwood mulch layered into the planting beds. A river rock spillway runs grade naturally through the space. The planting palette is built entirely for Central Texas conditions — ten species selected for drought tolerance, habitat value, and long-term texture.

Two Fires

Fire · 01

Backyard Pit

Welded Steel · Amber Glass

The back lawn fire pit runs long and low — welded 1/4-inch steel, filled with amber fire glass, LED-lit underneath so the base appears to hover. Set into the mulch bed between the bermuda lawn and the planting zone.

Fire · 02

Pool Pit

Welded Steel · Aqua Glass

The pool-side fire pit is square and close to the water — aqua fire glass reads against the pool surface, the steel floating on its recessed base at the edge of the teak deck. Two fires, two characters, one yard.

Night Mode

Full Yard · Fire + Water

After dark the pavers disappear into the turf joints, the fire pits glow in the background, and the pool reflects everything. This is the yard the client hired us to build — the one that makes you stay outside.

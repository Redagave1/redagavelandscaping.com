The Brief · Barton Creek

A real pool. Not a stock tank.

4 Weeks · Half the Cost of Gunite

The homeowners wanted a pool for cooling off in Austin’s 100°F+ summers and entertaining on weekends — but their lot sat on shallow limestone with limited excavation depth, and they didn’t want to spend $100K+ or wait 6 months for a traditional gunite build.

A shipping container plunge pool solved both constraints. The 20-foot modified ISO container arrived pre-lined with fiberglass, with plumbing penetrations factory-cut. Red Agave handled everything from excavation and structural pad through cedar cladding, marbled concrete decking, landscape lighting, and native planting screens — a complete outdoor living environment, not just a pool dropped in a yard.

The Raypak R8450ti heat/cool pump keeps the water at 82°F year-round: heating through Austin’s brief winters and — critically — cooling during July and August when unmanaged pool water hits 95°F and is too warm to be refreshing. Total project cost: under $48,000. Timeline: 4 weeks from excavation to first swim.

Cladding + Deck

  • —Western Red Cedar horizontal planks ($12/sq ft installed)
  • —Stainless steel furring + concealed fasteners
  • —Poured marbled concrete deck at $14/sq ft — 380 sq ft
  • —6-inch synthetic turf margins between concrete and pool edge

Lighting + Controls

  • —IP67 24V FCOB tape — underwater + deck perimeter

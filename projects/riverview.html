Design Intent · Austin, TX

A wall rebuilt. Concrete poured to look like marble. Fire that floats.

Cantera · Pavers · COB · Fire

The existing CMU block wall cut the backyard in half — the section bisecting the main space came out entirely. The wall that remained was redesigned rather than replaced. We welded an angle steel ledge to the top, then mortared 2×4-foot pieces of black cantera directly onto it. COB strip lights run beneath the steel so after dark the stone appears to hover on a warm line of light.

The paving is large format concrete poured to look like marble — the kind of surface that picks up wet weather and reflects the fire at night. Every concrete slab edge is framed in 1/4-inch welded steel and the whole field is sealed with Clemons sealer to protect the finish through the Austin seasons.

The fire pit is custom welded steel, set on a poured concrete curb with COB lights underneath illuminating the curb face — the same floating light detail carried from the wall to the fire. Black glass and black ceramic balls fill the burner bed so the flames lick up in a specific, deliberate pattern. The planting palette lines the borders: maiden grass, sky pencil holly, soft leaf yucca, variegated sedge, whale tongue agave, walking iris, artichoke agave, and soft caress mahonia.

Floating Pit

Welded Steel · COB Curb

Custom welded steel fire pit on a poured concrete curb, COB strip lights illuminating the curb face below. Black glass and black ceramic balls fill the burner bed — they shape how the flames move, pulling them into a deliberate lick pattern rather than a uniform burn. The steel floats. The fire performs.

Hardscape · 02

After Dark

Pavers · Fire · Planting

The concrete slabs pick up the fire and the low-voltage lighting from the beds. The steel frames each slab in a hard dark line. At night this yard has a completely different read than it does by day — and both are intentional.

Wall · 01

Cantera Ledge

Angle Steel · Black Stone

Angle steel welded to the existing wall, 2×4-foot black cantera mortared to the ledge, COB lighting running underneath. The remaining wall became the best feature in the yard — a material shift that makes the original CMU structure unrecognizable.

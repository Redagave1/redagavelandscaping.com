The Brief · Tarrytown

The yard disappears at 7 PM.

420 Linear Feet · 6 Zones

The hardscape was already in place — a mature Tarrytown lot with live oaks, existing flagstone paths, a steel-edged patio, and native planting beds. The problem was that the entire investment vanished after dark. Two low-voltage spotlights on the front oaks was the entire lighting package, and they pointed straight up into the canopy like searchlights.

Red Agave replaced the existing lights with a purpose-built system: 420 linear feet of 24V FCOB (Flexible Chip on Board) continuous ribbon lighting embedded in every steel edge and paver joint, 14 COB (Chip on Board) architectural uplights on specimen trees and stone walls, 4 diffused steel light boxes at seating areas, and pathway bollards along the front walk. Everything runs on 24V low-voltage through weatherproof transformers.

The entire system is controlled by two ESP32 WLED microcontrollers — one for the front yard, one for the back — running open-source WLED firmware. The homeowners control color temperature, brightness, scheduling, and scene selection from their phones. No proprietary app. No subscription. No hub.

The System

Not spotlights. Atmosphere.

Lighting · 01 The Network FCOB · COB · WLED Three lighting technologies working as one system. FCOB provides continuous glow along every edge — no hot spots, no visible diodes. COB delivers high-output directional wash on trees and walls. ESP32 microcontrollers running open-source WLED firmware tie everything together with WiFi control, scheduling, and scene presets — no proprietary gateway required.

System Details

FCOB Continuous Ribbon

  • —420 linear feet of 24V FCOB tape
  • —Embedded in steel edging channels + paver joints
  • —IP67 waterproof rating — rated for ground contact
  • —2700K warm white + tunable CCT zones

COB Architectural Uplights

  • —14 COB uplights on live oaks and stone walls
  • —CRI 90+ color rendering — true bark and stone color
  • —Adjustable beam angle: 15° spot to 60° flood
  • —Bronze-finished housings — invisible during the day

Controls + Distribution

  • —2x ESP32 WLED controllers (front + back zones)
  • —Open-source WLED firmware — no subscription or cloud dependency
  • —Scene presets: Evening Ambient, Entertaining, Pathway Only, All Off
  • —3x 24V weatherproof transformers, all conduit below grade

Light Boxes + Pathways

  • —4 diffused steel light boxes — welded on-site, frosted acrylic panels
  • —Soft architectural glow — no direct light source visible
  • —6 pathway bollards along front walk (Corten steel)
  • —Step lights at grade transitions — recessed into flagstone

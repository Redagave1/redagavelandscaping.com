What We Know About
Bee Cave

Soil & Terrain

Bee Cave sits on Edwards Plateau limestone and caliche soil — fast-draining, alkaline, and tough on traditional lawns. Our xeriscape designs work with this terrain, not against it, using native plants that naturally thrive here.

Water & Climate

With 300+ sunny days annually and Lake Travis drought restrictions in effect most summers, water-efficient landscaping isn't just smart — it's essential. Our designs routinely cut irrigation needs by 60% or more.

HOA & Permits

Many Bee Cave HOAs require architectural review for outdoor structures, decks, and significant landscaping changes. We handle permit coordination and know the approval process for the area's most active communities.

Native Plants

Texas sage, Mexican feathergrass, Autumn sage, Black-eyed Susan, and Agave are staples of our Bee Cave designs. These species thrive in the Hill Country climate, require minimal water, and look stunning year-round.
